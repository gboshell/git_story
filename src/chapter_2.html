Chapter 2

He was typing again, it had been a week since he had been forced to type something like this. Of course it came
back to him, lightning fast. He got back into the flow of things. Things had been going better, his
hygiene was much improved, his work- life balance, he thought was improving too. But there was
always the typing. He believed that one day it would stop, but that day was far off, so far that it was
better not to think of it. He pushed it to the back of his mind.

Yes, typing. Don’t think about holidays, weekend trips, weekends off or even nights off. If it wasn’t
typing it was something else, something that required immediate attention. Deadlines rose and feel,
one after another. They came, they went. The satisfaction of meeting a deadline washed away by the
constant wave that brought a new one, or two.

It was late. Well, not for a lot of people, but it was late for him. Eleven o’clock had crept up on him,
time to get everything ready for tomorrow. But the typing wasn’t finished. He would have to take a
break, take a break and come back to it. It was not going to go away.
